Has your council declared a nature emergency?

We are in a nature emergency. England is one of the most nature-depleted countries in the world. 35% of species populations have declined and 13% are threatened with extinction.

But there is still hope. Together, we can reverse these declines by increasing, improving and better connecting habitats and landscapes to create a brighter, healthier future for nature and people.

We’re urging all local authorities to declare a nature emergency

To help nature recover, we all need to act and local authorities have the power and influence to deliver the change nature desperately needs. Alongside local communities working to protect and restore nature, a growing number of local authorities are declaring nature emergencies. This is the first vital step to acknowledge the challenge.

Why declare a nature emergency?

Nature is integral to our culture, to a healthy society, and to a thriving and sustainable economy. Putting these plans into practice will show bold leadership in creating nature-rich places for people to live, work and play. It will also make a crucial contribution to the UK Government’s commitment to protect and manage 30% of UK land for nature by 2030.
