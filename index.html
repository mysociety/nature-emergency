We’re in a nature emergency

The UK nations are among the most nature-depleted in the world. But there’s hope. By acting together, we can restore nature, creating a brighter, healthier future for wildlife and people.

We’re urging all local authorities to declare a nature emergency

To help nature recover, we all need to play our part—and local councils hold the power to drive the change nature urgently needs. Alongside local communities working to protect and restore nature, a growing number of local authorities are declaring nature emergencies. This is an important first step in acknowledging the challenge.

To demonstrate commitment to meaningful action for nature’s recovery, we want to see declarations supported by:

Why declare a nature emergency?

Nature is integral to our culture, to a healthy society, and to a thriving and sustainable economy. Putting these plans into practice and working with communities and partners will show bold leadership in creating nature-rich places for wildlife and for people to live, work and play. It will also make a crucial contribution to the UK’s commitment to protect and manage 30% of UK land for nature by 2030, and to national species and habitats targets.
